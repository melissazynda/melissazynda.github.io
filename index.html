ABOUT ME

Hi! I'm Mel. I am a Product Manager at IBM, which is a fancy way to say that my job is to worry about literally everything: the personas to the copy, the responsiveness of the product to the business model canvas.

I define the roadmap and product strategy for a really cool experience within the Data and Analytics section of Bluemix, IBM's cloud platform. Most recently, my team and I integrated Jupyter notebooks into our Spark service, enabling data scientists and analysts to access wicked fast in-memory analytics in an interactive, media-rich environment. In previous roles at IBM (including UX research and design), I worked on Watson Analytics, Social Media Analytics, and taught hundreds of IBMers about design research and the IBM Design Thinking methodology.

When I'm not working, I like to play board games (Seven Wonders, Catan, Carcassonne, Dominion, that sort of stuff), read fantasy novels (the Rivers of London series is fairly new and very good, if you're looking for a recommendation), and try to keep my plants from dying, which is harder than it sounds because I live in Austin. When my brain feels mushy, I like to dabble in languages, particularly Croatian and Portuguese. I also loooove going to local poster shows because, come on, this is Austin after all, and I have to maintain the stockpile of prints that will live on my guest bed for what feels like an eternity until I get around to hanging them.

I made the switch to product management from design in the spring of 2015. When people ask what I do, I still prefer to say that I am a design strategist, as I think that best encapsulates my approach to problem solving. I made the switch because it seemed like the next natural step from my background in design research, and I wanted to be able to better affect the decision-making process. Boy, did I get what I asked for and then some! I do really enjoy it though, and I appreciate learning about and influencing all sides of the business.

Until relatively recently, I worked primarily as a design researcher in the IBM Analytics portfolio. Along with my background in sociocultural anthropology, I also have an academic background in design, so I worked quite a bit on that side of the house--a pretty even split between the two disciplines. My responsibilities as a researcher included conducting both generative and evaluative user research--including interviews, sketching exercises, card sorting, conducting surveys, concept and prototype walkthroughs, etc--along with creating design principles and research artifacts such as personas and journey maps that conveyed the insights from our users to the entire team. At the end of the day, research, to me, is about being a good listener, empathizer, and communicator.

As a designer, I have been responsible for everything from generating tons of initial concept ideas, through storyboarding, wireframing, being told "no" by dev and wireframing again, to creating functional specifications and polished final deliverables.
